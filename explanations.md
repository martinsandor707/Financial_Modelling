The structural dynamics of the global equity markets underwent an unprecedented stress test between February and April of the year 2020. Driven by the onset of the COVID-19 pandemic, the broader macroeconomic landscape experienced a synchronized shock that fundamentally altered historical correlations, volatility regimes, and sector performance metrics. Among the most profound anomalies observed by quantitative analysts during this period was the drastic compression of equity betas within the technology sector. This phenomenon is most notably illustrated by the rapid drop in the rolling beta of NVIDIA Corporation relative to the Standard and Poor's 500 Index (S&P 500).

The rapid compression of NVIDIA's beta from a highly aggressive posture down to a defensive 1.4 was not a reflection of declining corporate quality, nor was it a statistical error. Rather, it was a mathematical manifestation of a crash that affected the entire market as a whole.
